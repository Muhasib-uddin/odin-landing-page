Greatest Anime | THE BIG THREE Anime is a style of animated entertainment that originated in Japan. It covers various genres and is known for its distinctive art style, colorful characters, and diverse storytelling. Anime can include TV shows, movies, manga, and more. The "Big Three" refers to three highly popular and influential anime series from the 2000s: "Naruto," "Bleach," and "One Piece." These shows gained massive international recognition and played a significant role in popularizing anime globally."
Information About Big Three
"One Piece" follows Monkey D. Luffy, who gains the ability to stretch like rubber after eating a Devil Fruit. Determined to become the Pirate King, Luffy sets out on a journey with his diverse crew, the Straw Hat Pirates, to find the legendary treasure, One Piece. Along the way, they encounter powerful enemies, navigate treacherous seas, and explore mysterious islands. The series delves into Luffy's quest, the significance of friendship, the pursuit of dreams, and the mysteries surrounding the Grand Line.
"Naruto" follows the journey of Naruto Uzumaki, a young ninja with dreams of becoming the strongest and earning the title of Hokage. Born with a powerful nine-tailed fox demon sealed within him, Naruto faces discrimination but remains determined. Through training, battles, and friendships, Naruto progresses as a ninja. The story intensifies with the Chunin Exams, Sasuke's departure, and the emergence of powerful enemies. In "Naruto Shippuden," Naruto returns after training, facing deeper mysteries, the Akatsuki, and a Great Ninja War. The series explores themes of friendship, perseverance, and Naruto's quest to break the cycle of hatred while realizing his dream of becoming Hokage.
"Bleach" follows Ichigo Kurosaki, a teenager with the ability to see ghosts. After accidentally obtaining Soul Reaper powers, he must protect the living world from evil spirits known as Hollows. As he battles these malevolent entities, Ichigo uncovers a vast spiritual world, encounters other Soul Reapers, and learns about the existence of the Soul Society. The narrative expands with Ichigo's involvement in Soul Society politics, confrontations with powerful foes, and the revelation of dark conspiracies. Throughout the series, themes of friendship, duty, and self-discovery are explored.
